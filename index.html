Anakeesta

Mountain-top playground above downtown Gatlinburg. Plan 3-4 hours up top.

Things to do up there
  • Chondola or Ridge Rambler — scenic ride up the mountain (included)
  • Treetop Skywalk — 880 ft of hanging bridges, 50-60 ft up in the trees (included)
  • AnaVista Tower — 360° views of the Smokies, highest point downtown (included)
  • Hellbender Mountain Coaster — double-rail, 3,350 ft, banked turns (included)
  • Vista Gardens — botanical walking paths with sculptures (included)
  • TreeVenture Challenge — netted treehouses & rope bridges (included)
  • Firefly Village — mountaintop shopping & dining (included)
  • — — —
  • Rail Runner Mountain Coaster — single-rail, only one in US (paid extra)
  • Dueling Ziplines — race a friend down (paid extra, ~$60)
  • Gem Mining — kid-friendly fun (paid extra)
  • Astra Lumina — evening light walk (paid extra, separate eve ticket)

4:00 PM

Stroll the Gatlinburg Parkway

Anakeesta exits right onto the Parkway — perfect time to walk the main strip. Window shop, grab fudge or ice cream, browse moonshine tasting stops, find souvenirs. Plan ~1-1.5 hours of casual wandering.

Worth a stop on the parkway
  • The Village Shops — quaint European-style courtyard with 27 boutiques
  • Aunt Mahalia's Candies — fudge made on site, free samples
  • Ole Smoky Moonshine Distillery — free tastings, fun stop for photos
  • Mountain Mall — Smoky Mountain souvenirs all in one place
  • The Donut Friar — bach-photo-worthy donuts in The Village
Parkway
Parking tip: Park once near Anakeesta and walk the whole strip — way easier than driving in/out.
5:00 PM

Quick Pigeon Forge run (optional)

Pigeon Forge is the next town over — 10-15 min drive. Drive the Parkway, see the giant attractions lit up (Titanic Museum, upside-down WonderWorks, Hatfield & McCoy theater), grab photos at The Island. Easy add-on if the group's energy is up.

~9 mi · ~15 min from Gatlinburg The Island
Quick stops in Pigeon Forge
  • The Island — free to walk around, 200-ft Ferris wheel, fountains, shops, photo ops
  • Titanic Museum exterior — drive past the half-ship building, get pics
  • WonderWorks (upside-down building) — photo stop, you can't miss it
  • The Old Mill — historic working grist mill, cute shops along the river
Heads up on timing: If you do Anakeesta + parkway + Pigeon Forge, you'll be tight on time for a 6:30 dinner. Skip Pigeon Forge if anyone wants more parkway time, or push dinner to 7:00 PM.

Morning activity · pick one

Two options for the group. Decide as a group what fits the energy. Both are good — pick one.

Option 1 · The classic bach pick
🐴 Sugarlands Riding Stables

Affordable group horseback ride inside Great Smoky Mountains National Park. Beginner-friendly, gentle horses, scenic mountain trails through creeks. The big bach-photo moment.

Sugarlands Pricing
1-hour ride · 4 mi$50/person
1.5-hour ride · 6 mi~$65/person
Group of 10 · 1-hr ride~$500
~17 mi · ~30 min from Airbnb Navigate Book
Closed-toe shoes only. No sandals or flip-flops. Max weight 225 lbs. Long pants recommended.
Not riding? No problem. Sugarlands has picnic tables, restrooms, the river running alongside, and you can pet/photograph the horses. Non-riders can chill at the stables or walk down to Cataract Falls.
Option 2 · The chill bach pick
🎡 The Island in Pigeon Forge

Open-air entertainment complex. Free to walk around — pay only for what you do. Headliner: the 200-ft Great Smoky Mountain Wheel (~$18). Shops, fudge, mini bowling, ropes course, dancing fountains, food. Low commitment, easy group hang. Plenty of bach photo ops.
